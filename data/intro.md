### About me

I am a PhD student at the [Computer Laboratory](https://www.cst.cam.ac.uk/) at the [University of Cambridge](https://www.cam.ac.uk/), working with [Peter Sewell](https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/~pes20/) and [Neel Krishnaswami](https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/~nk480/). I work as part of the [REMS](https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/~pes20/people-new/index.html) group. 

I am interested in formally-rooted techniques and tools that improve the reliability of real-world software, and in the tension between the usability of such tools versus the strength of the guarantees they provide.

I currently work on [Fulminate](https://github.com/rems-project/cn), a tool that facilitates runtime-checking of separation-logic specifications to (gradually) improve assurance of real-world systems software written in C. 

Previously, I did an integrated Master's degree in Mathematics and Computer Science at [Imperial College London](https://www.imperial.ac.uk/), where I worked with [Dr Azalea Raad](https://www.soundandcomplete.org/) on formal transactional memory models for non-volatile memory.
